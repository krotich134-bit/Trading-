Risk Management Framework

Position Sizing

Fixed Fractional, Kelly Criterion (Quarter-Kelly), Volatility Targeting, CPPI, ATR-based

Portfolio Heat

H = √(rᵀΣr) — Correlation-adjusted risk aggregation with VaR and Expected Shortfall

Circuit Breakers

-5% daily → -20% monthly → -30% quarterly halt with mandatory review

Monte Carlo

Ruin probability estimation with block bootstrap for serial correlation

Recovery Mathematics: For drawdown D, required recovery G = D/(1-D). A 20% drawdown requires 25% gain; 50% drawdown requires 100% gain.

Safety Controls & Guardrails

Behavioral Safeguards

  • Cooling-off after consecutive losses
  • Anti-revenge throttle
  • Mandatory review tickets
  • No discretionary override of hard limits

Exposure Throttling

  • -5% drawdown → 50% size cut
  • -10% drawdown → 75% size cut
  • -15% drawdown → halt new risk
  • -20% monthly → full shutdown

Operational Governance

  • Immutable timestamped logs
  • Drift detection (PSI, KS tests)
  • Microsecond time sync
  • Signed audit artifacts
Human-in-the-Loop Mandate: No auto-execution. System produces hypotheses with invalidation logic. Humans approve/reject with recorded rationale.

Conclusion

This architecture targets probabilistic expected value, not guaranteed outcomes. Expected alpha is modest and fragile; costs, slippage, and correlation spikes can erase gross edge.

Long-run success depends on preserving capital through adverse regimes so compounding can continue.

"Edge detection is optional without robust risk controls; survival is non-negotiable."
